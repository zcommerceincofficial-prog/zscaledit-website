What 10x ROAS Means

Not a vanity metric. Real money.

10x ROAS means for every $1 spent on ads, a business gets $10 back in revenue. On a $3,000 wrap job that started with a $300 ad lead, that is the math.

Wrap shops scale faster than most businesses because one fleet account can be worth $50,000 to $200,000 per year. A single commercial client acquired through ads does not just mean one job. It means a relationship.

That is why we focus on the entire funnel, not just clicks. Clicks are cheap. Booked jobs that turn into long-term accounts are the real return.

Most other US markets remain open under the one-client-per-market model described on the about page.

What a 10x ROAS figure actually means

A 10x return on ad spend means every dollar spent on advertising generates ten dollars in revenue. For a vehicle wrap shop, this often means a $300 ad lead converting into a $3,000 job, and it compounds further when a single commercial fleet account, sometimes worth $50,000 to $200,000 annually, comes from a single ad-driven introduction. See the full acquisition process on the method page.

Why specific client names are not published

ZScaledIt's exclusivity model means each client is the only business in their market and niche with access to the agency's system, strategy, and creative. Part of that exclusivity is confidentiality: client logos and exact performance numbers are shared during a live audit call rather than published publicly, protecting each client's competitive position.
